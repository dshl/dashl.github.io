dreams with bare feet

here i am
in this place
the black crows call and the worms still crawl upon
my bare feet
as if beckoning

listen, listen
the police sirens swirl an eerie sound / while children / piercing the silence / like we
segregate and delegate
ourselves to the slashing of mr. murphy's tire
back when bullets sprayed through the department store left glass everywhere
D i a b l o s is still peeling himself off that old hag's sun–bleached home

the yellow bus has come to take us away
to build an edifice of things that were
a moment between sleeping and waking
where dreams tower over
the sun rises
and all things become known —
this is not our home
we are only wayfarers
in a strange land

wake up, wake up
it is time for us to sleep
for Mother is calling
