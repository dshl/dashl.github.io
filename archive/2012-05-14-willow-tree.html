Willow Tree

my willow tree has now grown branches
long enough to descend towards the earth
— reacquainted
with the roots that bulge beneath it all

the lake beside it is calm
but no one sees the flowering melancholy
and the mourning dew

I planted this willow tree
over my husband's grave
when Winter's first exhale
chilled our windowpane

cocooned in silence
rose and iris have sprung
but the willow is still barren
drooped over waiting for
blossoms to birth

the will was first to go
then his spirit
he followed the Ghost
to be baptized
but drank too deeply

so now
grief settles in
lies six feet under
my skin
and stays to gently kiss me in the morning
