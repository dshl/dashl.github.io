when we were young

when we were young, we loved to gaze at stars,
wondering who upon distant shores gazed like we — innocent,
with little minds and fingered hearts,
casting our net into a field of what could be to catch what dreams are made of
in a sea of stars.
Reality soon caught us,
strung the big dipper around our necks,
pulled us down deep to suffocate
and drown us like our parents before
who
dream no more,

Zombies
walking office hallways
unhappy,
trying to find a purpose in life.
Childhood left them
with the parent they waved goodbye to and never saw again,
the toys that soon found themselves
in yard sale bins to be collected by those who still speak half God —
their voices, tangled veins – bows
to melanin strings
tuned in perfect fits
intended only for God's ears.

Childhood left us
in our daydreams filled with fairy tales that we could stay forever young in never, never land,
somewhere between Nowhere and Goodbye
where
we jump upon bedroom springs through the galaxy and beyond,
and wait for our fairy Godmothers to whisk us away in pumpkin–grown chariots to the one we will forever love,
where
little boys and girls
still play
               hide
                        &
         seek
behind barriers
of blissful ignorance 'til darkness
fills them
with reflections of self, and
we forget our true selves.

So while we still can, let us set the night
aflame,
and stuff the ashes in our pockets,
recollect
the books our childhood was measured in,
for we do not wish on things that are not there,
but on things that were.
The stars are not empty space.

In the still quiet voice of the evening,
outstretch those limbs and
gaze once more
upon the night,
wondering who upon distant shores gaze like we.
